Visualisations of School Shootings in the USA

A Visualisation by Thomas Finlay

The number of deaths in America due to school shootings is tragic. The deadliest occuring at Sandy Hook Elementary school, Nebraska, where 26 people were killed in 2012.

The years back that the data shows can be adjusted using the slider above. It can be seen by sliding to the full range of years that large shootings annotated on the line chart are becoming more frequent. This is also reflected by the average deaths line increasing as the years are adjusted to more recent data.

Texas has remained the most dangerous state in recent times, however scrolling the slider back to 2001 and prior reveals that overall data shows California has the highest number of casualties.

Additional information can be found from the map by hovering over points indicating locations of shootings.

Victims in Private and Public schools

Hover over charts for additional information

90% of students in America attend public schools, however public schools make up 95% of the total school shootings in both Killed and Injured categories as depicted to the right. This means that public schools are statistically far more susceptible to school shootings compared to private schools.

Day and Time of Attacks

School shootings happen with varying frequency throughout the day on weekdays. There has not been a school shooting recorded on a weekend since 1999.

The chart to the right shows that shootings often happen first thing in the morning when students begin their school day, and there is a high frequency of shootings occuring in the afternoon of earlier week days.

Different types of Attacks by Shooters

Targeted and indiscriminate shootings are the top two most common shootings, the next section will delve into the details of each shooting type.

Surprisingly, accidental shootings are amongst some of the highest type of shooting, with 37 incidents being recorded since 1999. These incidences generally involve a student (often younger) bringing a firearm to school to show off and accidentally discharging it.
